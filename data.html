Data and Processing

The data used in these visualizations was taken from SF Fire Department Calls for Service. The data was first filtered by year to only show data from 2017, the year with the most incidents reported. The data was then filtered to include two specific incident types: medical incidents and traffic collisions. Both categories were compared to analyze their relationship to three different variables. The first variable was the top 30 neighborhoods with the most incidents reported. This narrowed down our data to include 30 categorical rows (each neighborhood) and one categorical column (medical or traffic incidents). The second variable we used was the top 9 unit types dispatched to the scene of the incident. For this our data included 9 categorical rows (each unit type) and again one categorical column (medical or traffic incidents). Each unit type was colored by if it was a non-life-threatening or life-threatening incident which added two more categorical rows to our data. We chose month as our final variable to show the recorded number of incidences per month. Thus, our data used 12 categorical rows (each month) and one categorical column (medical or traffic incidents). After the data processing, our dataset consisted of a total of 2 columns and 53 rows.
